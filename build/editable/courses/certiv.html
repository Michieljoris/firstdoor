Certificate IV in Training and Assessment

The Certificate IV in Training and Assessment qualification reflects the skill set suitable for creating a learning organisation. Completing the First Door elected units from a Certificate IV in Training and Assessment will assist those working in the Early Childhood sector who are Directors, Coordinators, Lead Educators or Educational Leaders.

Typically, those undertaking these units with First Door are working in Early Childhood and want to increase their skills in management and educational leadership.

What will you learn?

The Early Childhood National Quality Framework (NQF) is at the heart of our course content, and therefore student’s learning encompasses the NQF.

Students studying the Certificate IV in Training and Assessment with First Door will:

  • be equipped to follow legislation, service policies and meet the National Quality Standards.
  • assist you in developing your workplace into a learning environment.
  • assist you in promoting a culture of learning in the workplace.
  • enable you to role-model for inclusiveness and demonstrate professionalism.
  • manage work relationships and seek feedback from colleagues and clients.

Entry requirements

To enrol in this course, you will need to:

  • be currently employed in the registered Early Childhood centre.
  • have a sound understanding of the written and spoken English language.
  • have vocational experience.

Students need to have access to:

  • a computer (PC or Mac) with Internet and printer
  • Adobe Flash Player 7 and Adobe Acrobat Reader
  • Microsoft Word 97 or later (for PC)

Course curriculum

The Certificate IV in Training and Assessment is comprised of seven core units and three elective units. First Door currently offers three electives relevant to the National Quality Framework.

Course structure

At First Door, the three units which form part of the Certificate IV of Training and Assessment course are as follows:

  • TAEDEL301A     Provide work skill instruction
  • TAEDEL404A     Mentor in the workplace
  • BSBLED401A     Develop teams and individuals
