Diploma of Management

The Diploma of Management qualification reflects the role of individuals who are engaged to manage others, review processes and add value to their organisation. Completing the units offered from the Diploma of Management will assist those working in the Early Childhood sector who are Lead Educators or Educational Leaders.

Typically, those undertaking these units with First Door are working in Early Childhood and want to increase their skills in management and educational leadership.

What will you learn?

The Early Childhood National Quality Framework (NQF) is at the heart of our course content, and therefore student’s learning encompasses the NQF.

Students studying the Diploma of Management with First Door will:

  • develop capability to deliver quality and professional practice.
  • be equipped with skills to effectively recruit, select and induct staff.
  • gain skills and knowledge to develop your workplace into a learning environment.
  • be inspired to plan and monitor continuous improvement. 

Entry requirements

To enrol in this course, you will need to:

  • be currently employed in the registered Early Childhood centre.
  • have a sound understanding of the written and spoken English language.
  • have the required foundation skills and vocational experience.

Students need to have access to:

  • a computer (PC or Mac) with Internet access
  • a printer and scanner
  • Adobe Flash Player 7 and Adobe Acrobat Reader
  • Microsoft Word 97 or later (for PC)

Course curriculum

The Diploma of Management is comprised of eight elective units and First Door currently offers three electives relevant to the National Quality Framework.

Course structure

At First Door, the three units offered which form part of the Diploma of Management course are as follows:

  • BSBHRM402A    Recruit, select and induct staff

  • BSBLED501A     Develop a workplace learning environment

  • BSBMGT406A    Plan and monitor continuous improvement
