BSB51107 Diploma of Management

The Diploma of Management qualification will benefit the role of individuals who aspire to lead and develop people, review processes and add value to their organisation. Completing the units offered from the Diploma of Management will develop knowledge, skills and capability in those that work in senior or management roles in the Early Childhood sector. These units of study are also suitable for those that are experienced in their job role and are preparing their career for management roles.

 

What will you learn?

Students studying the five Diploma of Management units with First Door will:

  • develop capability to deliver quality and professional practice.
  • be equipped with skills to effectively recruit, select and induct staff.
  • gain skills and knowledge to develop your workplace into a learning environment.
  • be inspired to plan and monitor continuous improvement.
  • gain competence in mentoring, leading and developing teams and individuals.  

Entry requirements

To enrol in these units, you will need to:.

  • have a sound understanding of the written and spoken English language.
  • have the required foundation skills and vocational experience.

Students need to have access to:

  • a workplace environment for practical course-related activities
  • a computer (PC or Mac) with Internet access
  • a printer and scanner
  • Adobe Flash Player 7 and Adobe Acrobat Reader
  • Microsoft Word 97 or later (for PC)

Course curriculum

The Diploma of Management is comprised of eight units and First Door currently offers five units relevant to the National Quality Framework.

At First Door, the five units offered which form part of the Diploma of Management course are as follows:

  • BSBHRM405A    Support the recruitment, selection and induction of staff (elective) – workshop date 17th March

  • BSBLED401A     Develop teams and individuals (elective) – workshop date 28th April

  • BSBMGT406A    Plan and monitor continuous improvement (core) – workshop date 16th June

  • BSBLED501A     Develop a work place learning environment (core) – workshop date 21st July

  • TAEDEL404A     Mentor in the workplace (elective) - workshop date 18th August

 

We are committed to adding the three remaining Diploma of Management core units to our scope with ASQA, and plan to achieve this by September 2014. The remaining units that form the Diploma of Management course are:

  • BSBFIM501A      Manage budgets and financial plans (core)
  • BSBMGT502A     Manage people performance (core)
  • BSBMGT515A     Manage operational plan (core)
