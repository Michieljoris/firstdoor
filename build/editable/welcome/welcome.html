Open your opportunities, by learning with First Door

The benefits for Early Childhood educators, leaders or directors

  • You will be inspired to grow in confidence and capability.
  • Your learning is practical and relevant, relating to the National Quality Framework and your work role.
  • You will be working towards the latest nationally recognised qualifications, and developing professionally in your skills and knowledge.
  • New career opportunities will be available to you, and you will be able to inspire others.

But the best part? 

You will become well equipped to nurture young children, and their love of learning. By feeling capable, you will fully enjoy the rewards of your role, and be able to make a positive and real difference in people's lives.

That is why First Door has been founded with passion. It is our vision to develop capability in Early Childhood to make a positive and real difference for children, for families, for Early Childhood education and for you. 

 

Why choose First Door? Our big differences

First Door will connect, engage and support you in active learning with:

  • a personal mentor for study support and a monthly workplace meeting to guide you into achieving best workplace practice
     
  • engaging resources and social media environments for inspiration 
     
  • a competency based interactive workshop for each unit of study
     
  • meaningful workplace related assessment, with timely and constructive feedback on your work

     

2014 Scholarship opportunity

We are happy to have awarded our first two scholarship placements in February.

First Door is offering up to ten scholarship places during 2014 for students undertaking the new CHC50113 Diploma of Early Childhood Education and Care. Please note that scholarship applicants are NOT required to be certificate lll qualified, however applicants must be employed in a licensed Early Childhood centre, demonstrate motivation and commitment to their role according to the NQF and show potential as leaders in Early Childhood.
